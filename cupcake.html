Chinese Cupcakes


Image by my Mom


These Chinese cupcakes are pronounced as "Fa Gao" in Taishanese. It is a classic dessert made during holidays and celebrations such as Chinese New Year. They come in many different colors, but they're usually brown or light yellow depending on the type of sugar that was used. You can use brown or regular granulated sugar for this recipe. My mom always makes these cupcakes for family gatherings or just for fun. They're really easy to make and only require a large pot or wok to steam them.




Total Time: 40 min

Yields: 12 Cupcakes

Ingredients:




Ingredient: Amount:
Rice Flour 2 Cups
Brown Sugar or Granulated Sugar 1 Cup
Eggs 1
Baking Powder 2 Tablespoons
Water 1 Cup

Directions:



  1. Place a rack into a wok or pan. Fill the pot/wok with enough water till it reaches 2 inches below the rack and boil the water on medium heat.

  2. In a large bowl, sift the rice flour and baking powder. Crack an egg into the mixture and combine till if forms into a dough consistency.

  3. Pour a cup of water into a pot and bring to a boil. Add the sugar into the pot and mix until it has dissolved completely. Wait until a sugar syrup forms then turn off the heat.

  4. Pour the sugar syrup into the dough mixture and combine the two throughly with a spoon until a cake-like consistency forms.

  5. Pour the mixture into cupcake liners and place them onto the rack in the pot/wok. Cover and steam for about 30 minutes. Once it cools, serve and enjoy!
