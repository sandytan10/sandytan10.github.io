Mochi


Image by my Mom


Mochi is a traditional japanese dessert that is sold all around the world. It is made from glutinous rice flour and sugar. There are a lot of different types of flavors of mochi, but my favorite one has to be the regular. My mom became really interested in making mochi after her friend suggested her to try it. Ever since then, my mom has been experimenting different flavors and ratios to create the perfect mochi.




Total Time: 1 Hour

Yields: 18

Ingredients for the Mochi:




Ingredient: Amount:
Glutinous Rice Flour 2 Cups
Coconut Milk 1 Can
Milk 1/2 Cup
Sugar 1/2 Cup

Ingredients for the Filling:




Ingredient: Amount:
Peanuts 1 Cup
Shredded Coconut 1 Cup
Sugar 1/2 Cup

Directions:



  1. Place a rack into a wok or pan. Fill the pot/wok with enough water till it reaches 2 inches below the rack and boil the water on medium heat.

  2. In a large bowl, add a can of coconut milk, 1/2 cup of milk, and 1/2 cup of sugar. Mix the ingredients together until the sugar dissolves.

  3. Sift in the glutinous rice flour and use an electrical mixer to throughly combine the ingredients. It should create a thick liquid consistency.

  4. Sift the liquid mixture into a large pan and place it ontop of the rack. Cover and steam for half an hour.

  5. After steaming it, the mixture becomes a sticky dough. Remove the pan from the pot/wok and wait for 15 minutes until it cools.

  6. As the mochi is cooling, ontop of a piece of parchment paper, crush a cup of peanuts into small pieces. Combine it with the shredded coconut and sugar.

  7. Once the mochi has cooled, take a piece half the size of your palm and flatten it into a circle. Add a spoonful of the peanut filling wrap the edges of the mochi. Repeat this process and enjoy!
