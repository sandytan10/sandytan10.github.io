Soft Swerve




Image by Soft Swerve via TimeOut



Opened in 2016, this small ice cream shop located on the corner of Allen Street of Chinatown, Manhattan has attracted millions of people to try their signiture soft serve flavor: Ube. Ube is a yam that originated from the Philippines. Soft Swerve has attracted millions of people with their bright purple ice cream amongst thier other flavors. Currently, Soft Swerve offers 4 flavors: Ube, Macapuno Coconut, Hong Kong Milk Tea, and Black Seaseme. They also offer other seasonal flavors such as lychee and Vietnemese coffee.



Image by Soft Swerve via Best



The shop also offers more than a dozen toppings from mochi to fruity pebbles. Ontop of all of those options, there are 6 syrups to choose from. The possibilities are endless! If all of those choices overwhelm you, you can pick from one from thier set menu with interesting names such as Brooklyn Bridge (Black Seaseme ice cream with carmel drizzle, skor coffee, and mochi) and Broadway (Ube, fruity pebbles, and marshmallows). To top that off, Soft Swerve is considerably cheaper than other ice cream shops in the area. Nothing in their menu costs above $7! This is definitely the shop to go to if you're craving some ice cream and on a budget.



Location: 85B Allen St, New York, NY 10002
